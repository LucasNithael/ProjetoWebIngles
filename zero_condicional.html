Understanding conditional zero

What is Zero Conditional for?

Zero conditional is used when there is a result that always happens (fact). For example: "if you touch fire, you get burned".

Structure

The tense in Zero conditional will always be in the simple present, both for the 'if clause' and for the 'main clause':

If clause If + subject + verb in the simple present
Main clause subject + verb in the simple present

If the main clause comes before the If clause, the use of a comma will not be necessary.

Examples of conditional zero

If fish stay out of water, they die.
If I don't study for the exam, I can fail the subject.
We don't go out if the car is out of gas.
