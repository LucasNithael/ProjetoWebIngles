Understanding second conditional

What is Second Conditional for?

Second conditional is used to talk about things in the future that are probably not going to be true. For example: "If I won the lottery, I would buy a big house.

Structure

The 'if clause' will be in the simple past, and the 'main clause' will be would + base form of the verb.

If clause If + subject + verb in the simple past
Main clause subject + would + base form of the verb

If the main clause comes before the If clause, the use of a comma will not be necessary.

Examples of second conditional

If I met the Queen of England, I would say hello.
She would travel all over the world if she were rich.
She would pass the exam if she ever studied.
