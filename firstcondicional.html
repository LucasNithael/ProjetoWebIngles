Understanding first conditional

What is First Conditional for?

First conditional is used to talk about things which might happen in the future. For example: "If it rains, I won't go to the park".

Structure

The 'if clause' will be in the simple present, and the 'main clause' will be in the simple future.

If clause If + subject + verb in the simple present
Main clause subject + verb in the simple future

If the main clause comes before the If clause, the use of a comma will not be necessary.

Examples of first conditional

f I study today, I'll go to the party tonight.
If I have enough money, I'll buy some new shoes.
She'll be late if the train is delayed.
